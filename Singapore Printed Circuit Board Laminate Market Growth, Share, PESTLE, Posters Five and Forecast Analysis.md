Lamination is a technique wherein the material is permanently assembled by heating, pressuring, welding, or by use of adhesives. This technique improves the properties of a material such as strength, durability, appearance, stability, and others. Printed circuit board (PCB) laminate involves lamination of a circuit with a non-conductive material. PCB laminates connect and support the electronic components using pads, conductive tracks, and other features etched from copper sheets laminated onto a non-conductive substrate. PCB laminations are widely used in one copper layer PCB, two copper layer PCB, or outer & inner layer PCB.
